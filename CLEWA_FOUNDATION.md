# PART ONE: THE SOUL

## What Clewa Actually Is

Overseas manufacturing is one of the most complex, opaque, high-stakes environments 
a brand founder ever enters. You don't know the rules. You don't know the language. 
You don't know who to trust. You don't know what questions to ask. You don't know 
what you don't know — and the cost of getting it wrong isn't just money. It's time, 
relationships, reputation, sanity, and sometimes the business itself.

Finding a factory is hard.
Understanding how to order is hard.
Understanding what to order is hard.
Understanding how the order gets there is hard.
Payment is confusing.
Trusting a company you've never met is hard.
Incoterms are a foreign language.
Time zones, culture, communication — all of it creates friction.
And there's no map. No guide. No one watching your back.

Clewa is the complete confidence layer for that environment.

Not just a factory finder. Not just an order tracker. The infrastructure that makes 
an entirely foreign, unstructured world navigable — at every stage of the journey.

It gives you knowledge, structure, confidence, trust, and efficiency.
So you don't lose your money. Your time. Your relationships. Your sanity. Your business.

## The Three Stages — One Journey

Every brand moves through the same journey. Clewa serves all three stages:

**SOURCE**
"I don't know where to start and I'm scared of getting it wrong."
Find manufacturers. Understand the landscape. Ask the right questions. 
Vet properly before a single dollar moves. Get confident before you commit.

**OPERATE**
"I'm running orders but I don't know if I'm doing this properly."
Structure every order. Protect every payment. Get guidance at every decision point.
Know what to do before you need to ask. Never miss a deadline.

**GROW**
"I want to build something that lasts and make decisions from real data."
Track factory performance. Build institutional knowledge. Use your own order history 
to make smarter decisions. The platform gets more valuable with every season.

# PART TWO: THE CUSTOMER

## Who They Are

Physical product brand founders across fashion, apparel, accessories, footwear, 
and soft goods. Manufacturing overseas — primarily Vietnam, Bangladesh, Indonesia, 
China. From 300 units to 50,000 units. From first order to twentieth season.

Three types, one platform:
- Small DTC brand, 1-3 people, first factory, under $1M revenue
- Growing fashion brand, small team, 2-5 factories, $1M-$10M revenue
- Established product brand, dedicated ops person, multiple seasons running

## The Emotional Reality

The brand founder wiring their first deposit to a factory in Vietnam is doing 
something genuinely frightening. $4,500 leaving their account based on a WhatsApp 
conversation and a PDF they made in Canva at midnight. No contract. No protection. 
No clear recourse.

The anxiety of waiting for samples.
The helplessness of week 10 when something seems wrong.
The panic of missing a cargo cutoff.
The anger of receiving defective goods with no paper trail.

These are the moments Clewa prevents. Not metaphorically. Specifically.

## What They Say When They Describe It

"It's like having a sourcing director — it watches everything and tells you what to do."
"It's the professional infrastructure for your factory relationship."
"It's how serious brands manage overseas manufacturing."
"It's the tool that replaced my spreadsheet, WhatsApp, and guesswork."

# PART THREE: THE BRAND

## The Name

**Clewa** — from "clew," the ball of thread Ariadne gave Theseus to navigate the 
Labyrinth. The word "clue" derives from "clew." The original clue was the thread.

## The Mythology

Ariadne gave Theseus the thread to navigate the Labyrinth. Without it he enters 
a complex, opaque, potentially deadly system and gets lost. With the thread he 
navigates successfully, comes out the other side.

The labyrinth = overseas manufacturing.
The thread = Clewa's guidance, structure, and intelligence.
Theseus = the brand founder.

## The Thread as Design System

The thread is not just a concept. It is a literal visual element that travels 
through the entire platform — marketing site and application. It is golden, bright, 
and purposeful. It guides you. It shows you where you are. It connects where you 
came from to where you're going.

Every time you see the thread in the UI, it means: you are here, this is the path, 
follow this.

## The Brand Line

**"The thread through your manufacturing labyrinth."**

## The One Sentence

**"Clewa gives brands the thread to navigate overseas manufacturing."**

## Who Built This

This feels like it was built by three people simultaneously:

A **fashion founder** who understands the customer from the inside — who has 
ordered from overseas factories and felt every one of these problems personally.

A **tech founder** who builds systematic, precise, feature-complete infrastructure — 
who thinks in data models, edge cases, and scalable architecture.

An **operator** who has been on the factory floor — who knows what PP sample means, 
what happens when the AQL fails, what Tet does to a production timeline.

The product should feel like all three. Warm enough to be human. Precise enough 
to be trusted. Grounded enough to be credible.

# PART FOUR: THE VOICE

## The Tone

**Direct.** No hedging. No "consider" or "might want to." Say what you mean.

**Specific.** "AQL 2.5 on a 300-unit order allows 7 defective units" not 
"quality standards are important." Real numbers. Real consequences.

**Honest.** About what we do and don't do. About what the real risks are. 
About what happened in real orders.

**Warm but not casual.** Founder-to-founder. Not customer service. Not corporate. 
Not startup-bro. The person who's been through this and wants you to get it right.

**Protective.** We're on your side. We're watching. We'll tell you before you ask.

## What We Say

"You'll find out in week 4, not week 10."
"Your money never moves without your approval."
"Structured orders. Protected payments. Built-in guidance."
"The questions experienced buyers always ask — answered before you need them."

## What We Never Say

Seamless. Powerful. Game-changing. Revolutionary. Robust. Cutting-edge.
World-class. Innovative. Hassle-free. End-to-end solution.

We don't use SaaS filler words. Every word earns its place.

## The Copy Principle

**Features tell. Outcomes convert.**

Don't say: "Milestone-gated payment system with configurable release conditions."
Say: "Your deposit releases on PO issue. Bulk can't start until you approve the sample. 
Final payment is blocked until QC passes. Nothing moves without your confirmation."

# PART FIVE: THE DESIGN LANGUAGE

## The Philosophy

**Three tensions, one resolution:**

Editorial (fashion) + Precise (infrastructure) + Warm (mythology) = Clewa

Editorial: the scale, the confidence, the whitespace, the intentionality.
Precise: the grid, the consistency, the information density, the functionality.
Warm: the golden thread, the human voice, the founder-to-founder energy.

These are not contradictions. Every great fashion brand's e-commerce site resolves 
exactly this tension. Clean and precise but never cold. Warm but never soft.

## The Thread — Visual System

The golden thread is the single most important visual element in the system.
It is not decorative. It is structural. It means: you are here, this is the path.

**Where it appears:**

1. **Navigation** — a vertical golden line runs down the left edge of the sidebar 
   (app) and connects nav items. The active item has a bright gold dot on the thread.

2. **Order timeline** — the production stages (PO → Sample → Production → QC → Ship) 
   are connected by a horizontal thread. Completed stages are gold. Upcoming are gray.
   This is the most important UI element in the entire application.

3. **Section labels** — a short horizontal thread line (6px height, 24px width) 
   appears before every section label on marketing pages.

4. **Progress** — every multi-step form uses the thread as the progress indicator.

5. **Hero** — on the homepage, the thread appears as a visual element running 
   through or adjacent to the headline. It leads the eye.

6. **Alerts and guidance** — proactive AI alerts have a golden left border. 
   The thread marks: pay attention here.

## The Color System — Starting Fresh

**THE THREAD:**
- Thread active:    #D4940D  — rich warm gold, for interactive thread elements
- Thread bright:    #F0A818  — vivid gold, for decorative thread / line elements  
- Thread on dark:   #EDAD3E  — lighter gold for dark backgrounds

**THE SURFACES:**
- Background:       #FFFFFF  — pure white. Clean. Precise. Editorial.
- Card:             #FAFAFA  — off-white cards on white. Subtle lift.
- Surface 2:        #F5F5F5  — for secondary containers, input backgrounds
- Dark section:     #111111  — near-black for high-contrast sections (protection, etc.)
- Dark card:        #1C1C1C  — cards on dark background

**THE TEXT:**
- Primary text:     #0D0D0D  — near-black. Not pure black. Warm.
- Secondary text:   #5A5A5A  — muted but readable. 5.1:1 on white.
- Disabled:         #A0A0A0  — clearly inactive
- On dark:          #F0F0F0  — near-white for dark sections
- On dark muted:    #909090  — muted text on dark

**THE STRUCTURE:**
- Border:           #E8E8E8  — visible but not heavy
- Border strong:    #D0D0D0  — for emphasis
- Divider:          #F0F0F0  — very subtle row separation

**STATUS COLORS** (small use only, never as backgrounds):
- Success:          #16A34A  — green for passed/confirmed/shipped
- Warning:          #D97706  — amber for attention needed (close to thread, distinct)
- Critical:         #DC2626  — red for payment blocked/QC fail/urgent
- Info:             #2563EB  — blue for informational only

**The rule:** The golden thread is the ONLY warm color in the system. 
Everything else is neutral. This makes the thread unmissable and meaningful.

## Typography System

**One font. Inter. Always.**
The editorial quality comes from scale, weight, and spacing — not a personality font.
Fashion editorial design uses heavy sans-serif all the time: System Magazine, 032c, 
Buffalo Zine. The font is not what makes it editorial. The way you use it is.

**The Scale:**

| Name       | Size | Weight | Tracking    | Use |
|------------|------|--------|-------------|-----|
| hero       | 56px | 700    | -0.04em     | Homepage H1 only |
| display    | 44px | 700    | -0.03em     | Major section heroes |
| h1         | 36px | 700    | -0.025em    | Page titles |
| h2         | 28px | 600    | -0.02em     | Section headings |
| h3         | 22px | 600    | -0.015em    | Card titles, subsections |
| h4         | 18px | 600    | -0.01em     | Labels, emphasized items |
| body-lg    | 16px | 400    | 0           | Hero sub, key paragraphs |
| body       | 14px | 400    | 0           | Standard body text |
| body-sm    | 13px | 400    | 0           | Secondary descriptions |
| caption    | 12px | 400    | 0           | Metadata, timestamps |
| label      | 11px | 600    | 0.08em      | ALL CAPS section labels |
| micro      | 10px | 500    | 0.06em      | Tags, status badges |

**Line heights:** 1.08 for hero/display, 1.15 for h1-h3, 1.4 for body, 1.3 for captions.

## Spacing and Layout

**The grid:** 12-column. 24px gutters. Max content width 1200px.
**Base unit:** 4px. All spacing is multiples of 4.
**Section padding:** 96px top/bottom desktop, 64px mobile.
**Container:** max-w-6xl (1152px) standard. max-w-7xl for full-width sections.

**The editorial principle:** Don't fill every column. Whitespace is intentional.
A section with text on the left and nothing on the right for 6 columns is fine.
That emptiness creates breath. It signals confidence.

## Component Philosophy

**Cards are for interactive units. Not for sections.**

A card is something you can click, expand, or take action on. A card is a discrete 
unit. Sections of content are not cards — they are sections. Use whitespace and 
borders to separate, not card containers.

**The three card types:**
- Card (white, subtle shadow): primary interactive units — order cards, factory cards
- Surface (off-white, border): secondary content — feature descriptions, info blocks
- Dark card (dark bg, dark border): for dark section content

**Borders:** Single pixel, #E8E8E8. Never double. Never colored except for 
the golden thread left-border on alerts.

**Border radius:** 8px standard. 12px for large cards. 4px for small elements. 
0px for structural dividers and the thread elements themselves — the thread is precise.

**Shadows:** Very subtle. `0 1px 3px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)` standard. 
`0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)` for elevated interactive elements.
No dramatic shadows. No glows. Except: a very subtle golden glow on the primary CTA 
button: `0 0 0 3px rgba(212,148,13,0.15)`.

## Buttons

**Primary:** Background #D4940D, text white, 0px border radius... no.
  Actually: Background #0D0D0D (near-black), text white. 
  The golden thread does not appear as a button fill — that would be too much.
  The primary button is bold and dark. The thread appears around it (golden glow 
  on hover).

**Secondary/Outline:** Border #E8E8E8, background transparent, text #0D0D0D.
  On hover: border becomes #D4940D (the thread appears).

**Ghost:** No border, no background, text #5A5A5A. 
  On hover: text #0D0D0D.

**Sizes:**
- Default: height 36px, padding 0 16px, text 14px
- Large: height 44px, padding 0 20px, text 15px  
- Small: height 28px, padding 0 12px, text 12px
- Icon: 36px × 36px

# PART SIX: THE PAGE ARCHITECTURE

## Navigation Structure

**Marketing nav (logged out):**
Primary: Source · Operate · Grow · Pricing
Secondary (More): How it works · Trade tools · Why Clewa · For Factories · FAQ

**App nav (logged in, brand):**
Orders · Factories · Intelligence · Resources
(Secondary: Settings, Help)

**Logo:**
The letter "C" in the golden thread color on a near-black background.
Or: "Clewa" wordmark in near-black with the thread line accent.
NOT: an amber S box. That is Sourcery. This is Clewa.

## The Eight MVP Pages

These eight pages must be fully resolved before any user signs up:

1. **Homepage** — makes them feel understood before they read a word
2. **Pricing** — makes the decision easy
3. **Auth** — removes friction from signing up
4. **Onboarding** — shows them exactly what to do first
5. **Dashboard** — the daily-use screen, must be calm and clear
6. **Create Order** — the core action, must be guided and reassuring
7. **Order Detail** — where they spend their time, must be information-dense
8. **Intelligence** — the differentiating feature, must feel like a conversation

Everything else — Studio, Compliance, Analytics, Trade Tools, FAQ — is secondary.
These can be rough at launch. The eight above cannot.

## Page-by-Page Intent

**Homepage:** The visitor arrives lost or uncertain. They leave understanding exactly 
what Clewa is, who it's for, and what to do next. The headline makes them feel seen.
The three stages (Source/Operate/Grow) show them where they are in the journey.
The primary CTA is "Start free — no credit card."

**Pricing:** One clear decision: Starter (free) or Growth ($49/month or $399/year).
Scale exists but is not the focus. The page explains what unlocks at each tier 
through outcomes, not feature lists.

**Auth:** Clean split screen. Left: the headline + three bullet benefits + 
"Free for your first order." Right: the form. Minimal. No noise.

**Onboarding:** Three questions. Two minutes. "What are you making?" / 
"What's your factory situation?" / "Invite your factory or start with our directory."
The completion screen is specific to their answer.

**Dashboard:** A new user sees one thing: their situation-specific roadmap.
An active user sees: their orders in a clean list, the thread timeline showing 
status at a glance, and one proactive alert if anything needs attention.
Nothing more.

**Create Order:** Four steps. Each step has a clear name in plain English.
Every field has a one-sentence explanation. Guidance cards appear where needed.
The thread progress indicator connects the four steps.

**Order Detail:** The order is the hero. Status is the first thing. 
The thread timeline shows where the order is in the journey. 
Messaging, documents, payments are in tabs. The AI proactive guidance 
appears at the top when something needs attention.

**Intelligence:** Left panel: what the AI is watching (order signals, health).
Right panel: conversation. The AI speaks first with the most important thing.
Not "how can I help" — "here is what needs your attention today."

# PART SEVEN: THE IMPLEMENTATION RULES

## Rules That Cannot Be Broken

1. **The thread is always golden.** Never use the thread color for backgrounds, 
   surfaces, or fills. Only for: the line itself, icons, text emphasis, borders 
   that signal attention.

2. **One page at a time.** Every build session touches one page. Screenshot before. 
   Screenshot after. Three changes max per session.

3. **No blind building.** If you cannot see the result, do not make the change.
   Use screenshots. Review before merging.

4. **Copy earns every word.** No filler. No jargon. Every sentence serves the customer.

5. **The empty state is not an afterthought.** Every page that can be empty 
   (dashboard, orders, factory list) needs a designed empty state that tells the 
   user exactly what to do next.

6. **Mobile is a first-class citizen for factories.** Factory users access Clewa 
   on mobile. Every factory-facing screen must work perfectly at 375px.

7. **No fake data. No aspirational stats.** Every number cited must be real 
   and attributed. "94% OTIF — from completed orders, not self-reported."

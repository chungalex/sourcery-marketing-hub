# PART ONE: THE PHILOSOPHY

## The Problem

Most physical product brands manage their most important business relationship — the one with their factory — on WhatsApp.

Not because WhatsApp is good at it. Because nothing better exists for them.

The enterprise tools cost $50,000 to $500,000 per year and require dedicated IT teams. They are built for corporations with sourcing departments, not for a founder doing 500 units of a denim jacket in Ho Chi Minh City.

The sourcing marketplaces show you factories but have no infrastructure for working with them once you have found one. They are phone books, not operating systems.

The production management software — Katana, MRPeasy, Craftybase — is built for companies that make things themselves. In-house manufacturing. Shop floors. None of it serves a brand that has a factory in Vietnam making their product for them.

So the founder of a $2 million fashion brand manages a $150,000 annual production relationship on WhatsApp, a Google Sheet, and memory. Spec changes agreed in chat are never documented. Payment releases happen on trust. Quality disputes have no evidence trail. Missed seasons happen because nobody was watching the right signal at the right time. And when something goes wrong — and something always goes wrong — the brand has no leverage and no record.

This is not a niche problem. This is how the entire category operates. Every DTC brand, every fashion label, every product founder who does not own their factory manages this way. There are hundreds of thousands of them globally. They collectively represent billions in annual production value managed through tools that were never designed for this purpose.

The gap is not a gap in features. It is a gap in category. Nobody has built the professional infrastructure for this specific relationship — the brand-to-overseas-factory relationship — at a price and complexity level that serves the actual customer.

Until Clewa.

## The Belief

We believe that the factory relationship is the most important, most fragile, and most undermanaged asset a physical product brand has.

Everything depends on it. The product quality. The delivery timing. The cash flow. The customer promise. The brand reputation. Every single thing a physical product brand sells to its customers starts as a relationship with a manufacturer somewhere in the world.

And yet most brands treat this relationship with less professional infrastructure than they give their email inbox.

We believe this is not because founders are careless. It is because the infrastructure did not exist. Nobody built it for them at the right price, the right complexity level, and with the right understanding of how this relationship actually works.

We also believe that the knowledge gap is as damaging as the infrastructure gap. Most brand founders doing their first production run do not know what they do not know. They do not know that approving bulk before a formal PP sample confirmation is a mistake they will pay for. They do not know that choosing DDP incoterms with an inexperienced factory creates customs exposure. They do not know that an AQL standard of 4.0 instead of 2.5 means they are accepting twice as many defects. They learn these things by making expensive mistakes.

We built Clewa so they do not have to.

We believe that a first-time brand founder should be able to make decisions like someone who has done this twenty times before — not because they have the experience, but because the platform gives them the knowledge at the exact moment they need it.

We believe that the factory relationship should compound in value over time. Every completed order should make the next order smarter. Every factory interaction should build a permanent record that protects the brand, scores the factory, and calibrates the intelligence layer. The platform should get better the more you use it — not because of product updates, but because of your own order history.

We believe that factories deserve better tools too. A serious factory that delivers consistently, passes QC, and responds professionally has no way to prove that to Western brands beyond self-reported claims. Clewa gives them a verified performance score built from real completed orders. Their OTIF rate is their reputation, and it compounds with every order they run through the platform.

We believe the category of "factory relationship infrastructure" does not yet exist as a recognised software category. We are building it. That is both the opportunity and the responsibility.

# PART THREE: THE PRODUCT

## What Clewa Does — The Complete Lifecycle

Clewa covers every stage of the factory relationship:

**Find** — Factory marketplace with registered manufacturers, AI matching, due diligence checklists, OTIF scores from real order data

**Vet** — Factory verification badges, audit request system, certification tracking, OTIF performance scoring, factory review system

**Quote** — Multi-factory RFQ system, structured quote comparison, AI quote normalisation (flags apples-vs-oranges quotes)

**Contract** — Auto-generated production contracts at PO stage, tied to platform-documented conditions

**Order** — Guided 4-step PO creation with contextual guidance at every field, tech pack upload, milestone structure

**Pay** — Milestone-gated payment releases (deposit → sample gate → QC gate → final), payment history permanently logged

**Sample** — Sample submission workflow, photo annotation, revision rounds with factory acknowledgement, formal approval gate

**Produce** — SKU tracking, production photos, factory milestone check-ins, timezone approvals, change order flow with formal acknowledgement

**QC** — Defect reports with AQL enforcement, defect taxonomy, corrective action requests, final payment gate

**Dispute** — Formal dispute filing with evidence trail, payment blocking mechanism

**Ship** — Carrier tracking, freight document checklist, shipment documentation

**Comply** — CSDDD, UFLPA, Modern Slavery Act documentation export

**Reorder** — Intelligence from real lead time history, safety stock calculator, reorder date calculation

## The Intelligence Layer

The platform is not just a system of record. It watches every order and speaks before being asked.

**Level 1 — Rule-based (built, works now):**
Five specific triggers that fire when conditions are met:
1. Silent factory — PO issued, no response in 3 days
2. Delivery window compression — days remaining vs production stage mismatch
3. Payment gate checklist — documents missing before payment release
4. Spec conflict — PO specs vs tech pack notes conflict
5. Overdue stage — order in current status longer than typical

**Level 2 — Pattern-based (requires 5-10 orders per factory):**
Factory-specific historical patterns in alerts. "This factory typically responds in 4 hours — 3 days of silence is unusual."

**Level 3 — Predictive (requires 50+ completed orders):**
Seasonal patterns, capacity signals, quality predictions from accumulated data.

## The Guidance Layer

At every decision point in the platform, contextual guidance appears:
- Before choosing a factory: due diligence questions, what documents to request
- Before issuing a PO: tech pack completeness, incoterms recommendation, AQL explanation, Tet warning
- During production: what this delay means, what to message the factory
- At QC: options, leverage, what each choice means for the relationship

The sourcing director experience — delivered contextually, not in help articles.

## The AI Workspace (Production Intelligence)

A full dedicated page at /intelligence. Not a chatbot. A thinking partner.

Left panel: what the AI is watching — live order signals, health status, critical alerts
Right panel: conversation — AI opens first with specific current situation, not "how can I help"

System context injection on every message: all active orders, factory names, statuses, delivery windows, critical alerts. The AI knows the operation. Every response is specific to the brand's real situation.

## The Order Room (Tier 2 Build)

Each order becomes a living workspace with tabbed navigation:
Overview → Timeline → Messages → Documents → QC → Payments → Shipping

Only tabs relevant to current status are active. Progressive disclosure — draft shows 4 components, closed shows review and reorder. The platform organises the production relationship, not the user.

## Factory Notes

A living document per factory, auto-generated from real order data.
When an order closes: actual lead time, defect rate, revision rounds, QC result automatically appended.
Manually editable: quirks, contacts, lessons learned, seasonal patterns.
Only visible to the brand, not the factory.
After 5 orders: irreplaceable institutional memory. Switching cost becomes real.

## Mobile-First Factory Interface

Factories access Clewa primarily on mobile. The factory dashboard is optimised for touch:
- Large touch targets for production milestone check-ins
- Photo upload for production progress
- Quick message interface with Vietnamese input
- Order signal cards with one-tap responses

## The Communication Philosophy

Clewa does not compete with WhatsApp by importing WhatsApp data. Clewa competes with WhatsApp by being genuinely better for production communication.

Every message is attached to an order — timestamped, attributed, permanently documented.
Real-time Vietnamese↔English translation with manufacturing context.
Order context visible during messaging — both parties see the PO, spec, milestone state.
Every approval is a formal one-click sign-off, not "ok looks good" in chat.
Read receipts tied to factory OTIF score — response time is part of performance.
Payment is tied to platform communication — the milestone release requires documented thread.

# PART EIGHT: THE SHOPIFY ROADMAP

## Why Shopify matters
Clewa knows production. Shopify knows sell-through. Together they close the complete loop:
- Shopify tells Clewa what's selling and how fast
- Clewa calculates exact reorder point from real sell-through + real lead time
- Clewa surfaces "issue FW26 PO by July 3 or you'll stockout in October"

This is the sourcing director. Not metaphorically — actually. One automated system doing what a $100K/year hire does.

## Build sequence

**Phase 1 — CSV import (build now, 2 days)**
Brand exports Shopify sales CSV (built-in Shopify feature). Uploads to Clewa. AI parses weekly velocity per product. Safety stock calculator auto-populates. No OAuth, no Meta review, no partner approval.

**Phase 2 — Full OAuth (build after 10+ CSV users)**
Complete Shopify app with OAuth. Real-time sales velocity. Automatic reorder calculation. Pre-populated PO creation. Requires Shopify Partner account and app review.

**Phase 3 — Shopify App Store**
Distribution channel that generates inbound from brands already looking for production tools. 4M+ active Shopify merchants. "Production management" is a real search query in the App Store.
